Expert App

Introduction

The Expert app is a social platform that allows people to connect to Experts of nearly any field by the means of their own preferred method of communication.

Problem Statement

In the current age of technology people have their own preferred way of communication. Our users need a way to easily communicate their questions in a centralized format.

Goals

Create a format that allows users to have full control of how communication is handled within the features of the application.

User Research Analysis

Before beginning the design process I needed to meet with potential users to learn about their habits and emotions regarding applications that were in the same field as or adjacent to my project.

With this information I’ll be able to synthesize the information into developing the user personas for this project.

By using affinity mapping I was able to categorize their responses which allowed me to discover shared pain points and needs that will help direct my research.

  • Communication preference completely varies across each participant
  • Common expectation is having options
  • Participants felt more willing to participate in discussions when others were involved.
  • Users only hold on to subscriptions when they feel they are getting their value’s worth.

Customization may be key in discovering a solution.

User Personas

Using the results of the research analysis I was able to generate two personas who closely represented the targeted demographic for the project. Young and busy people who may not have time to research a problem.

Journey Maps

Using our user personas I was able to create journey maps that allowed me to empathize with Chen and Alaina by determining their motivations for their behaviors.

Task Flows

Now that I have my user personas I am able to identify paths and decisions my users may take when completing a task.

Site Map

I created my own site map and compared it with the results of a card sort test from a group of participants.

I recruited 5 participants whose experience with applications varied. It was important to get all sides of the spectrum in how they would form and expect navigation to look like from applications they may see in their day-to-day lives.

The results helped produce and refine a site map which will dictate the format of the design going forward.

Wireframes

Once I had collected all my research and data I could begin creating wireframes and prototyping the visual design for the app.

Usability Testing

With a prototype created I was ready to present my design for evaluation.

The goal was to test for ease of use when navigating through the main features with the expected outcome to discover any problems that I may have overlooked so far.

Test Objectives

  • Are users capable of navigating the application just by memory of familiar design?
  • Could submitting an inquiry be faster or easier? Is there anything missing? e.g. additional filters?
  • Are screens efficient enough to provide users a clear understanding with navigation?

I executed both moderated in-person and moderated remote testing. The study involved 6 participants who were in their mid-20s of various occupations that were recruited through local and online networking.

Issue: Participants were confused by the main functionality of submitting a question
Suggested Change: Make alterations to the titles for forms and selections.
Evidence: Much like the previous issue, participants who were stuck at this step asked questions about what each function would do rather than initially understanding.

Issue: Participants had issues understanding the functionality of the Delivery Preference screen.
Suggested Change: Make alterations to the titles for forms and selections.
Evidence: The participants who lingered longer on the screen before taking action asked questions about the purpose of each option. It seems necessary to take another look upon improving for a better understanding.

Design System

I wanted to create a cohesive theme for the application that stayed consistent and simple across the entirety of the design. I referred to WCAB to ensure that the theme remained accessible for as many users as possible.




Developing the Expert app was quite the journey for me. I wanted to make sure I had a full connection with my potential users in every step of the way with researching, testing, and surveys to ensure that the application was user centric based and I believed I achieved that with the results I have so far.

A lot of the process was very new to me as it was happening and now that the project is finished I could see a lot of things I would’ve done differently. For example my interview process could be refined as I felt I wasn’t getting the answers that could help largely improve the user experience.

To push the app further along however I would want to apply more user testing with the current version of the application and make additional changes. The UI design in the final mock up has not been finalized through testing. The forum feature has not been implemented yet and it would need user flows, functionality testing, and more.
